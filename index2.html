🌐 Spotlight Topic: Phishing Scams

What is Phishing? Phishing is when attackers trick you into sharing sensitive information (passwords, credit card numbers) by pretending to be a trustworthy source, like your bank or a well-known company.

How to Spot a Phishing Attempt:

  • Suspicious URLs: Hover over links before clicking. Is the web address slightly off (e.g., "amaz0n.com")?
  • Urgent Language: "Act now or lose access!"—legitimate companies rarely use scare tactics.
  • Generic Greetings: Emails like "Dear User" instead of your name could be a red flag.
  • Unexpected Attachments: Don’t open files unless you trust the sender.

What to Do:

  • Delete suspicious emails or messages.
  • Use a password manager to avoid reusing passwords.
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) for extra security.

🔒 Tool of the Month: Password Managers

Why You Need One: A password manager stores your passwords securely and generates strong, unique passwords for every account. No more sticky notes or repeating “Password123”!

Top Recommendations:

  • LastPass
  • Dashlane
  • Bitwarden (Free & Open Source)

Start by securing your most sensitive accounts: email, banking, and social media.

📊 Quick Stat:

Did you know? 91% of cyberattacks begin with a phishing email. Staying vigilant can save you from becoming a statistic!

🧠 Cyber Literacy Challenge:

Test Your Awareness:

If you receive an email saying your account is locked and asking you to click a link to reset your password, what should you do?

  1. Click the link and reset your password immediately.
  2. Ignore the email completely.
  3. Visit the official website directly and check your account status.

(Answer: #3)
